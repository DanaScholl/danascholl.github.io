About Dana


Dana Scholl (born June 2, 1995) is an American actress and humanitarian. She has received an Academy Award and a Golden Globe Awards, and has been cited as Hollywood's highest-paid actress under the age of 25. Scholl made her screen debut as in Divergent (2014). Her film career began in earnest with her first leading role in a major film, 4 Minute Mile (2014). Scholl earned her Academy Award and Golden Globe award for her leading role in Mr.Robot (2016). She has taken a break from film and has focused on television, starring as Dr. Amy Lewis in the ABCs medical drama titled Greys Anatomy. She is rumored to be nominated for the 2017 Emmy Award.

In addition to her acting career, Scholl is noted for her humanitarian efforts, for which she has received Noble Award, among other honors. She promotes various causes, including women's rights, health & medicine, homeless assistance and animal protection. Her work with Make-A-Wish Foundation has become a worldwide phenomenon and Scholl has pledged to donate a percentage of her income to the organization annually.

As a public figure, Scholl has been cited as one of the most influential and powerful people under 25 in the American entertainment industry. Her personal life is the subject of wide publicity, although Scholl is noted as being a private person when it comes to her relationships, including her family and love life.

Early Life

Born in Chicago, Illinois Scholl is the daughter of lawyer Jeff Scholl and actress Ann Scholl. She is the sister of baseball player Eric Scholl. On her father's side, Scholl is of German and Polish descent, and on her mother's side, she is of primarily Polish ancestry. Her paternal great-grandparents and maternal grandparents immigrated to the United States.

After four years in Chicago, the Scholl Family moved to Madison, Wisconsin to be closer to Ann Schollâ€™s parents and raise their children in a smaller town. Scholl attended Madison East High School, participating in the theater program, along with local theater productions.

Education

Scholl attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison from 2013-2014. She studied Creative Writing and was an integral part of the theater department, starring in all three musical and plays during her time at the university. She also performed at the Overture Center for the Arts.

Beginning of Career

Feeling like she wanted to take her talent to the screen, Scholl moved to Los Angeles with her mother in the hopes of making it big. Kirsten Cleary, acting agent with over thirty years of experience, immediately saw talent in Scholl, signing her up for acting booth camp at The Acting Company.

Scholl started booking commercial gigs, appearing in video advertisements for PetSmart and Old Navy. Within two months of moving to Los Angeles, Scholl booked the part Rita in Divergent and within three months, Scholl appeared as the lead in the major motion picture 4 Minute Mile as Claire Jacobs.

Philanthropy

Scholl has taken part in several charity events. She has supported charities such as the Make-A-Wish Foundation, National Alliance to End Homelessness and StandUp for Kids. On March 10, 2015, Scholl announced she would be joining the Make-A-Wish Foundation as their celebrity liaison and has become the face of the organization. When asked about being the face of the Make-A-Wish Foundation, she stated "Being a part of this amazing organization is truly an honor. I am excited to make a difference in the lives of these very deserving children. While I am technically the face of the organization, do not think I am just the poster child. I am in the office weekly, helping to grant wishes. I try to attend local hospitals a minimum of once a week. It is the least I can do." Additionally, Scholl has pledged to donate a portion of her income annually to the cause.

Scholl has also been a huge participant in different Hollywood galas in support of many causes.

Personal Life

In February 2016, Scholl purchased a $2 million four-bedroom, 4-bath mansion in an upscale gated community in Malibu, California. Scholl has been in a relationship with Zac Efron since late 2015. The two went public about their relationship during an interview Efron had with Extra in March of 2016. They ended their relationship in June 2016. They reconciled in August 2016.
